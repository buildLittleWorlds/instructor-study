The Second Opinion

Who owns an app when AI helped make it?
Thesis. AI assistance does not erase authorship. The important question is not "Did AI touch this?" but "Who chose the direction, tested the behavior, revised the result, explained it, and took responsibility for publishing it?"

Course boundary: this page is an instructor demonstration. It collects no ratings or data and requires no experiment.

Who is the author of the glider?

Before the layers settle into place, take the field's own hardest case. The glider below is running for real — five cells that rebuild themselves one square over, four generations at a time. Four claims about it. Assign each one.

A real B3/S23 board with wrapped edges, so the glider loops forever instead of dying in a corner.

How the field actually answered. Conway authored the rule — searched out by hand, 1968–70, on Go boards and graph paper. Richard Guy found the glider in 1969, the way a naturalist finds a beetle, while the group tracked the R-pentomino: nothing in B3/S23 mentions motion, and nobody drew the glider in. Bill Gosper's MIT team engineered the gun in 1970 to win Conway's $50 wager. And the glider on your screen just now ran because you loaded a page that pressed the rule into service. The field never crowned one author. It did something more useful: it named contributions — rule by Conway, discovered by Guy, constructed by Gosper — which is exactly what an authorship note does.

The coda that belongs in a writing course: Conway spent decades in a love/hate relationship with the invention that eclipsed his deeper mathematics (he said so himself). Made things outgrow their makers. The authorship note is how a maker stays responsible anyway.

The paper layer

What made me curious

I wanted to know whether a person can still be the author when AI writes most of the code.

What to notice

The stronger note does not minimize AI. It names the human choices that shaped the published result.

What I would try next

I would compare authorship notes from different kinds of projects: games, stories, simulations, and research tools.

What I chose

I kept the note comparison and the five-layer Game of Life example, while removing the old judge-versus-panel experiment.

The authorship-note forge
