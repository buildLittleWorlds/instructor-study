What made me curious

The word bank can mean a river's edge or a place that holds money. I wanted an interaction that lets a reader feel how surrounding words help settle which meaning fits.

What to notice

Transformer attention allows information at one token position to connect with information at other positions inside the permitted context. Those connections help create context-sensitive representations: the same word can behave differently in different sentences.

Last session's Life lab ended on three design dials that separate a cellular automaton from a transformer: neighborhood, rule, and iteration. This page opens the first dial and looks inside. A Conway cell's neighborhood is fixed, local, and binary — the same eight cells, each counting exactly one, in or out. Attention's "neighborhood" is chosen, global, and graded — any word in the window, weighted by relevance, different for every word and every sentence.

A counter-model, not an equivalence. A Conway's Game of Life cell checks eight immediate neighbors, and influence travels farther only through repeated steps. A transformer token can connect to a distant position through attention in a single layer. A transformer is not a cellular automaton, and a CA neighborhood is not attention.

How this page was made

Loading…
Accuracy note. Three things on this page must not be confused. The glowing cues are a hand-authored teaching diagram, not model data. The blue closeness bars are real cosine similarities of isolated word embeddings — baked for the curated sentences and computed in-browser for the sandbox — but they show context-free closeness, not attention weights: real attention compares learned query/key projections in context, across many heads and layers. And the sharpness slider demonstrates a softmax; it is not Session 2's sampling temperature and its yellow shares are not extracted from a model.

What I tried next

I wired a separate sandbox to the live in-browser model from Session 1, so a reader can type a sentence and compute real word similarities on the spot without disturbing the curated lesson. I also added a sentence whose strongest teaching clue, “wings,” sits twenty positions from “bat”: the local step counter has to crawl the whole distance before attention crosses the gap in one.

What I chose and how AI helped

AI helped with: drafting the page's code and modules, proposing candidate sentences and swaps, and computing the cosine-similarity table with the regeneration script recorded in this session's README.

I chose: the sentence pairs, the three swaps and the different lesson each one teaches, which context cues the diagrams highlight, the framing of neighbors versus attention, and the decision to show the model's real numbers beside my hand-drawn cues rather than pretend either one is the other.

The spotlight shows where a reading gathers its evidence. In a trained transformer, nobody types a rule saying "river" must matter to "bank." So how does the model acquire that rule? That is next session's question.
