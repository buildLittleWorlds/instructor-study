# Session 5 Instructor Paper-App: The Next Move

The Bridge artifact for Session 5: **a curiosity is useful when it gives you a next move.** Genre: history walk + triage forge + letter stamp. The reader predicts the fate of the R-pentomino before running a real Life engine, watches Conway's group's curiosity become the field's direction (glider → $50 wager → Gosper's gun), triages four authored curiosities into four verdict categories — every chip shows the future that filing buys — then stamps their own letters onto the board and finds out what happens, and finishes on three sentence-starters aimed at their own project. Landing line, on the page and in class: **"A useful curiosity gives you a next move."** Fully self-contained: one file, no fetch, no dependencies; works opened straight from `file://`.

## The three modules

1. **the five cells nobody could predict.** A real bounded B3/S23 engine (120×80, double-buffered `Uint8Array`). Predict-first gate: the run controls stay locked until the reader picks a settle-time chip. Run (~20 gen/s), Fast (~120 gen/s), Skip to the end (chunked so ~1,100 generations never freeze the page, progress every 100 gens), Reset. Live readouts: generation, population, a population sparkline, and a **real glider census** — sixteen templates the page generates at load by running a lone glider four steps and taking every rotation/reflection, matched exactly with an empty one-cell border, every ten generations. Settle detection is real too: state hashed and compared with two generations back (checked only after gen 10, hash confirmed cell-by-cell). On this board the R-pentomino settles at **generation 1103, population 118, peak 314, most gliders aloft at once 5** — computed live on every visit, never hardcoded; the reveal card quotes the canonical infinite-board record (1,103 generations, 116 cells, six gliders; Richard Guy, 1969) as history beside the live numbers. Second beat: **Load the glider gun** swaps in the Gosper gun and the $50-wager story; on this bounded board the gliders die at the edge, and the page says so.
2. **does it give you a next move?** Four authored curiosity cards, one per verdict category: **watchable now / buildable smaller / just an opinion (as stated) / needs a yardstick first**. The chips never lock. The first click on a card is scored ("Same call." / "I filed it differently."), and every click — first or rival — swaps in that verdict's own authored filing, sixteen in all, so clicking the losing verdicts shows what each filing actually buys you. The instructor's filing carries the flag *"— this is where I file it."* Card 3's yardstick filing names Session 6 (the same tag convention the Trailheads cards use); card 4 files as watchable now and hands off to the letter stamp with a scroll button. No tally — the segment is an argument, not a quiz.
3. **the seed is a knob.** The Bench's move demonstrated on the instructor's own app. The framing on the page: the five-cell seed is a knob chosen almost without thinking, and your letters are a different setting of the same knob. An input takes up to four letters (live-sanitized to A–Z), and **Stamp the board** maps them through an authored 5×7 pixel font onto the *same* engine — no second board, no new machinery. The room guesses out loud (burns out / settles into ash / still going at five thousand), then runs with the existing controls. The settle reveal shows that seed's own computed numbers with the R-pentomino prediction verdict and the canonical record suppressed, and closes on the no-record line plus the Bench handoff: change one thing in something already built, say your guess first, and the comparison does the analysis. Reset returns to the R-pentomino — and if no predict chip was ever picked, Reset re-locks the run controls, so stamping letters can never leak past the predict-first gate.

The page closes with **Your next move** — the three sentence-starters ("I want the visitor to… / The interesting part is… / The smallest next version is…") answered in the reader's head, nowhere else — and an orange pointer to the Trailheads Bench, the page's one outbound handoff.

## Honesty framing

- **Computed, live:** every generation of the board, the settle detection, the glider template matching (and its "most seen aloft at once" maximum), the sparkline, the R-pentomino prediction verdict, and the letter stamp (font pixels become live cells, nothing else). The readouts always show this board's own numbers; the canonical record appears only as quoted history.
- **Authored:** the three history beats, the four curiosity cards and their sixteen filings, and the pixel font's letterforms. All of it lives in one marked block at the top of the page's script (`EDIT HERE`).
- **Limits, stated on the page:** the board is bounded (edges absorb travelers; infinite Life is the ideal object the canonical numbers describe); the census recognizes only the standard glider's sixteen phase-orientations, so rarer spaceships go uncounted; the run is fully deterministic — the surprise is not randomness; and "settled" means period ≤ 2, so a rarer longer-period oscillator would honestly read as "still going."
- **Guardrail:** a transformer is not a cellular automaton, and the page makes no LLM mechanism claims. Session 5 teaches a continuity — curiosity → direction — and says so plainly.

No localStorage, no exports, no class data: typed letters become cells and are forgotten; the reader's picks sharpen the reader's eye and go nowhere.

## Using it in the 15-minute Bridge

1. **Predict.** Put the five cells on screen and make the room commit to a chip before anything runs — under 100, a few hundred, over a thousand, never. Wrong-but-locked beats right-but-after.
2. **Run, then Skip.** Let it churn at Run speed for ~20 seconds so they feel the 1970 problem, then Skip to the end and read the reveal card aloud: settle generation, peak, gliders — and Guy finding the glider by watching.
3. **Load the gun.** Tell the $50 story over the running gun; let the census count the stream. A strange seed is a curiosity; the gun is what a curiosity becomes when it hands someone a next move.
4. **Triage: vote, click, compare.** Have the room vote a verdict aloud before you click, then click the room's chip and read the future that filing buys. Then click yours — the flag marks it — and argue the difference. Cards 1 (AI art: opinion → narrowed build) and 2 (virtual city → one intersection) are the reliable arguments; card 3's yardstick filing plants Saturday's flag.
5. **Stamp a volunteer's initials.** Card 4 cashes out one section down: take a volunteer's letters, make the room guess the fate out loud, Run, then Skip. Read the reveal — the board's own numbers, no record book — and say the bridge sentence: this is the same move the Bench asks of your app tonight.
6. **Land the sentence-starters.** "I want the visitor to… / The interesting part is… / The smallest next version is…" — then release students to pick their own direction: a current app, an older app, or a new curiosity, plus one deployed improvement. If anyone asks what abandoning a direction looks like done honestly, tell the retired-research-machine story aloud — it is no longer on the page; the line to land is *abandoning a direction is a research move when you can say what you kept.*

## Before class

- The page is fully self-contained — no data folder, no fetch. Every authored piece (history beats, the four cards and sixteen filings, the letterforms) sits in the `EDIT HERE` block at the top of the inline script; swap cards freely, keep the four verdict categories. If you touch the seeds or the engine, re-check the settle numbers by watching a Skip run — the page computes them fresh, so the reveal card will stay honest even if the copy in this README goes stale.
- **Tested letter seeds** (settle generation, computed on this board 2026-07-21): `DP` ashes almost instantly (gen 11) — a good joke at your own expense; `JD` gen 19; `AB` gen 658; `CAT` gen 803; `EL` gen 1,545; **`HI` runs 2,017 generations — past the R-pentomino's own 1,103 — before settling at population 185**. A single `C`, `S`, or `X` **burns out to an empty board** by gen 9. No tested seed was still running at 5,000 — that chip stays as the honest "didn't settle" outcome, and the Skip path reports it plainly if a student's letters ever earn it.
- Pre-run one long seed (`HI` or `EL`) so you know how the Skip feels, and keep a burner (`X`) in your pocket for the burns-out fate.
