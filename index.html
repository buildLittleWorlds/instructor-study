Instructor Examples

Paper-apps that make one AI idea visible at a time
These are demonstrations and idea generators. Student projects begin with a curiosity, one working interaction, a short explanation, and a public URL. Students do not have to copy an example's topic, data, method, or technical complexity.

 Idea 1 · Embeddings and prototypes

The Map of Meaning

A real neural network draws a map of words and places a reader's word on it. Roam the geography and inspect the center of a cluster.

 Idea 2 · Probability and temperature

Where Does Randomness Enter?

Separate a system's starting state, update rule, and execution-time randomness, then compare them with language-model sampling.

 Idea 3 · Attention and context

Attention Spotlight

Change the visible context, guess which words settle an ambiguous meaning, and reveal hand-authored teaching cues.

 Idea 4 · Training and learned behavior

The Slop Detector

Inspect a computed comparison of repeated vocabulary in an illustrative sample. The page demonstrates an instructor method but collects no class data.

 Idea 5 · Evaluation and robustness

The Agreement Machine

See how named criteria change a judgment, then transfer the lesson to one concrete success check for a student-built app.

 Idea 6 · AI evaluation and authorship

The Second Opinion

Compare an apology for using AI with an authorship note that documents direction, testing, revision, and responsibility.
